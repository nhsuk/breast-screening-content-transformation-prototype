Breast screening (mammogram)

How to book or change an appointment - Breast screening (mammogram)

You will be sent an invitation letter in the post when it's time for you to have breast screening (mammogram).

Booking your appointment

Your invitation letter will either:

  • ask you to book an appointment by phone, email or sometimes online
  • give you a pre-booked appointment, and tell you when and where to come for breast screening

Breast screening may be done at:

  • breast screening clinic, often within a hospital
  • mobile breast screening unit – these could be in lots of different locations, such as in a supermarket car park

You will usually get instructions on how to find the clinic or screening unit.

Information:

Try to book your appointment as soon as you get invited.

If you have not been sent a letter

If you have not been invited for breast screening by the time you are 53 and think you should have been, contact your local breast screening service.

Find breast screening services

Things to tell the team when you book

To help them plan your appointment properly, it's important that you tell the breast screening unit if you:

  • have a disability
  • have problems with your mobility – wheelchair access is available at most centres and some mobile units
  • have breast implants – you may need an extra X-ray to get a clearer picture around the implant
  • are pregnant, think you could be pregnant or are breastfeeding
  • are under the care of a breast consultant
  • have had a mammogram in the last 6 months
  • need an interpreter if your first language is not English
  • have had a coronavirus vaccine within the past 5 days – some people may have swollen glands in their armpit which can be picked up by the X-rays

You can have breast screening whatever size or shape your breasts are.

Booking if you are trans or non-binary

You should have breast screening if you:

  • were assigned female at birth and have not had surgery to remove your breasts and male chest reconstruction (top surgery)
  • were assigned male at birth and have been talking feminising hormones for longer than 2 years

Find out more about when you’ll be invited and who should go.

If you are registered with a GP as a man, you may not be invited automatically.

If you think you should have breast screening, but you are not invited automatically, talk to your GP surgery or call your local breast screening service to book.

Find breast screening services

There are things that may help when booking an appointment:

  • someone else can book the appointment for you if that is more comfortable for you
  • you can ask for an appointment at the beginning or end of a clinic to give you more time and privacy
  • you can speak with the breast screening unit directly, you do not have to be referred by a GP

Changing or cancelling an appointment

If you need to change or cancel your appointment, contact the breast screening service you booked your appointment with.

If you were sent a booked appointment, your letter will tell you how to change or cancel your appointment.
